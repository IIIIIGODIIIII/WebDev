Chicken Enchilada

These are the best chicken enchiladas ever! They are creamy and cheesy and full of flavor. The recipe is very easy to make and can be adjusted to suit your taste. You can add more or less of any ingredient to make it your own. Enjoy!

  • 1 (10.75 ounce) can condensed cream of chicken soup
  • 1/2 cup sour cream
  • 1/2 cup chopped green onions
  • 1 tablespoon chili powder
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 2 cups chopped cooked chicken breast meat
  • 1 cup shredded Cheddar cheese
  • 1 cup shredded Monterey Jack cheese
  • 1 (15 ounce) can black beans, rinsed and drained
  • 1 (4 ounce) can diced green chiles
  • 1/2 cup chopped fresh cilantro
  • 12 (6 inch) corn tortillas
  • 1/4 cup milk
  • 1/2 cup shredded Cheddar cheese
  • 1/2 cup shredded Monterey Jack cheese
  1. Preheat oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C).
  2. In a medium bowl, combine the soup, sour cream, green onion, chili powder, and garlic. Mix well.
  3. In a separate large bowl, combine the chicken, Cheddar cheese, Monterey Jack cheese, black beans, green chile peppers, and cilantro. Mix well.
  4. Divide the chicken mixture evenly among the tortillas, placing it in a line down the center of each tortilla. Roll up the tortillas and place them seam side down in a 9x13 inch baking dish.
  5. In a medium bowl, combine the milk and 1/2 cup Cheddar cheese. Mix well and pour this mixture over the rolled tortillas.
  6. Sprinkle the remaining 1/2 cup of Monterey Jack cheese on top of the sauce.
  7. Bake at 350 degrees F (175 degrees C) for 30 to 35 minutes, or until the enchiladas are heated through and the sauce is bubbly.
